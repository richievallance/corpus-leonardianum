**[1r-C] ZAHNRÄDER (GEARS) — CLOCKWORK**

**Italian Original:**
```
De denti delle rote

Le rote dentate non osservano una medesima natura di forza nel voltare che la no fanno de' lor charegli. Perchè alcuna volta fanno forza col fondo de' lor denti sopra lo stremo del dente del carello, e alcuna volta fanno forza collo stremo de' lor denti sopra il fondo de' denti d'esso carello. Sichè per quessto, essendo adoperato tal forza di rota in varie distantie dal suo cientro, ella adopera con varie potentie. E per quessto il tenpo delli orilogi, ancora che lle rote fussino condotte perfettamente, nondimeno no[n] sarà tal tenpo d'equal moto.
```

**English Translation:**
```
On the teeth of wheels

Gear wheels do not maintain the same nature of force in turning as they do with their pinions. Because sometimes they exert force with the base of their teeth on the tip of the pinion's tooth, and sometimes they exert force with the tip of their teeth on the base of the pinion's teeth. Therefore, this force of the wheel, being applied at various distances from its center, operates with various powers. And for this reason, the tempo [escapement] of clocks, even if the wheels were made perfectly, will nevertheless not have such tempo of equal motion.
```

**CRITICAL FOOTNOTE — TERMINOLOGY:**
> "Italienisch tempo im Sinne von deutsch Hemmung (engl. escapement, französisch échappement): siehe weitere Belege auf fol. 5v, 8r, 9v, 27c, 48v und öfter."

**English:** Italian "tempo" in the sense of German "Hemmung" (English escapement, French échappement): see further evidence on fol. 5v, 8r, 9v, 27c, 48v and more often.

**CROSS-REFERENCES:**
- Zahnradstudien: fol. 3v, 5r (see Inhaltsübersicht)
- "carello/caregli" = Ritzel (pinion, smaller gear)
- Manni/Biffi Glossario 2011, p.78: "una delle due ruote di un qualunque accoppiamento rotoidale"

**DISCREPANCY NOTED:**
The German translation uses "Ritzel" (pinion) but Leonardo's Italian uses "carello" — a term not widely attested elsewhere in the mechanical vocabulary. This suggests either:
1. Regional Milanese dialect term
2. Leonardo's own coinage
3. Specialized clockmaker's vocabulary

**CORRELATION WITH WHITE PAPER:**
This passage establishes the **varying drehmoment (torque) problem** that the entire Madrid I regulatory system addresses. The central shaft doctrine requires constant power delivery despite variable tooth engagement.

**[1r-D] TRANSMISSION COMPARISON**

**Italian Original:**
```
De rota e corda e denti

Usasi in ne' moti alcuni con denti, alcuni con corde, alcuni con contatti. De' quali quelli de' denti sono i più potenti. Dopo questi sono quelli delle corde. Il terzo più debole è cquello del contacto. De' denti acade in orilogi e in simili strumenti. Delle corde filatoi e ssimili. Del contatto sicome ne' torcitoi della seta.
```

**English Translation:**
```
On wheels, cords, and teeth

In motion, some use teeth, some use cords, some use contact. Of these, those with teeth are the most powerful. After these are those with cords. The third weakest is that of contact. Teeth are used in clocks and similar instruments. Cords in spinning wheels and similar. Contact as in silk twisters.
```

**CRITICAL FOOTNOTE:**
> "Eine ähnliche Systematik für Hilfsmittel zur Übertragung drehender Bewegung kennt auch die jüngere mechanische Fachliteratur noch. Vgl. Barth, Maschinenelemente 1915, Abschnitt 5."

**English:** A similar systematic classification for devices transmitting rotary motion is still known in later mechanical engineering literature. Cf. Barth, Machine Elements 1915, Section 5.

**CORRELATION:** This establishes Leonardo's **three-tier transmission hierarchy**:
1. **Teeth** (highest power, lowest slip) → clocks, precision instruments
2. **Cords** (medium power, medium slip) → spinning wheels
3. **Contact/friction** (lowest power, highest slip) → silk twisters

This hierarchy directly informs the **Da Valenca V3 Layer Model L4 (Distribution)** power transmission doctrine.

**[1v-F] EISENTECHNIK: BRÜNIEREN UND ÄTZEN**

**Italian Original:**
```
A ffare ferro brunito che pare dipinto di machie azurre

Fa lle tue piastre del ferro brunite e sottile di qualunche figura ti piace. E cque[sta] posa sopra i carboni infocati dall' oposita parte del suo brunito e vedrai tal ferro farsi prima di color giallo, e poi a ppoco a poco si converte in azurro, e poi si farebbe nera nel tropo stare. Oppure quando è a un colore azzurro che ti piaccia, e ttu allora subito la leva di su' carboni e llasscia freddare. Di poi dipigni di biaca a olio sopra esso brunito fatto azuro quello che tti piace, e llasscia secare per 2 o 3 di. Po' metti soto l'aceto. Allora l'azuro toco dallo acieto si partirà e rimarrà il primo brunito, e cquella parte che ffia ocupata dalla biaca rimarrà azurra. La qual biaca leva col sapone tenero, e pò lava con acqua fressca e assciuga con banbagia e rimarrà bella. E 'l simile si pò fare ne l'oricalco apun[to].
```

**English Translation:**
```
To make browned iron that appears painted with blue spots

Make your iron plates browned and thin in whatever shape you like. And place this on the hot coals on the opposite side from the browning, and you will see this iron first become yellow, then gradually convert to blue, and then it would become black if it stayed too long. Or when it reaches a blue color that pleases you, then immediately remove it from the coals and let it cool. Then paint with white lead in oil on the browned surface made blue whatever pleases you, and let it dry for 2 or 3 days. Then put it in vinegar. Then the blue touched by the vinegar will depart and the first browning will remain, and that part occupied by the white lead will remain blue. Remove the white lead with soft soap, then wash with fresh water and dry with cotton and it will remain beautiful. And the same can be done exactly with bronze.
```

**CRITICAL FOOTNOTE:**
> "Nachtrag zu unbestimmter Zeit. Die Brünierung dient dem Korrosionsschutz, aber wie hier auch dem Dekor; vgl. Hansgeorg Hofmann / Jürgen Spindler, Verfahren in der Beschichtungs- und Oberflächentechnik, 2. Aufl., Leipzig 2010, Kap. Brünieren und Metallfärben. Bleiweiß (biacca a olio): basisches Bleikarbonat, diente als wichtige Ölfarbe der Künstler. Hier fungiert das Bleiweiß allerdings als temporäre Beschichtung, um die zu erhaltenden gebläuten Dekorflächen während des Wegätzens der übrigen gebläuten Flächen abzudecken."

**English:** Addition at an indefinite time. Browning serves corrosion protection, but as here also decoration; cf. Hansgeorg Hofmann / Jürgen Spindler, Processes in Coating and Surface Technology, 2nd ed., Leipzig 2010, Ch. Browning and Metal Coloring. White lead (biacca a olio): basic lead carbonate, served as an important oil color for artists. Here, however, the white lead functions as a temporary coating to protect the blued decorative surfaces to be preserved during etching away of the remaining blued surfaces.

**SIGNIFICANCE:** This is a **complete metallurgical process** for decorative iron/bronze work. Note the precision:
- Temperature sequence: yellow → blue → black
- Timing: 2-3 days for oil-based resist to cure
- Etching medium: acetic acid (vinegar)
- Surface finish: cotton buffing

This appears **unrelated to clockwork** but demonstrates Leonardo's range across materials science. **Later addition** per footnote — suggests ongoing workshop notes integrated into the codex.

**CROSS-REFERENCE NETWORK:**
This establishes **rotation ↔ linear alternation** as a **central theme** running through at least 16 folios. This is the **core kinematic transformation** of the entire Madrid I system.

**PHILOSOPHICAL NOTE (Paragraph 1):**
"Every body demands its limbs, and every art demands its tools. And as soon as the whole is created, also its parts are created."

This is **not technical instruction** — it's **design philosophy**. Leonardo is stating that:
1. Form follows function ("body demands limbs")

This aligns with the **Da Valenca reading model**: Madrid I is not a parts catalog, it's an **integrated system** whose modules are interdependent.

### **DISCOVERY 2: CHIARAVALLE ABBEY CLOCK**

**Location:** f.10v-11r

**German Footnote:**
> "Diese und andere Teile der folgenden Seiten beschäftigen sich bevorzugt mit Elementen der Uhrenmechanik, die Leonardo teilweise im Sinne einer neuen Maschinenlehre zu verallgemeinern versucht, teilweise aber auch nutzt, um in Variationen Möglichkeiten zur Verbindung von Mond- und Sonnenanzeige einer Uhr zu studieren. Nur im Einzelfall (fol. 11v) bezieht er sich ausdrücklich auf die Turmuhr des Zisterzienserklosters Chiaravalle bei Mailand."

**English:**
> "These and other parts of the following pages deal preferentially with elements of clock mechanics, which Leonardo partly attempts to generalize in the sense of a new machine theory, but also uses in variations to study possibilities for connecting moon and sun displays of a clock. Only in individual cases (fol. 11v) does he expressly refer to the tower clock of the Cistercian monastery of Chiaravalle near Milan."

**Italian Original (f.11v):**
```
Poli in poli, come in Chiaravalle.
```

**English:** Shafts within shafts, as in Chiaravalle.

**CRITICAL CROSS-REFERENCE:**
> "Diese Uhr war ein wichtiger Auslöser für sein genaueres Studium der Uhrenmechanik. Er leitet viel anderes Material aus ihr ab, hatte freilich auch in Florenz schon Kontakt zu einem angesehenen Uhrmacher, zu Guiglielmo della Volpaia. Näheres bei Pedretti, Studi Vinciani 1957, S. 210-213, id., Leonardo architetto 1978, S. 316-319, und Taglialagamba, Automazioni 2010, S. 35-39."

**English:**
> "This clock was an important trigger for his more precise study of clock mechanics. He derives much other material from it, though he also had contact in Florence with a respected clockmaker, Guiglielmo della Volpaia. Details in Pedretti, Studi Vinciani 1957, pp. 210-213, id., Leonardo architetto 1978, pp. 316-319, and Taglialagamba, Automazioni 2010, pp. 35-39."

**SIGNIFICANCE:**
- **Specific clock identified**: Chiaravalle abbey tower clock
- **Location**: Cistercian monastery near Milan
- **Feature**: Concentric shafts (for independent hour/minute hands or sun/moon displays)
- **Function as model**: Leonardo derives multiple mechanisms from this single reference clock

**DATING ANCHOR:**
The Chiaravalle clock is a **datable physical object** that Leonardo had direct access to. This is stronger than textual cross-references — it's a **material witness** to the codex's Milanese period composition.

**CORRELATION WITH WHITE PAPER:**
The white paper identifies Chiaravalle as a dating anchor, but this footnote network establishes it as the **methodological source** for Leonardo's systematic clock studies. He's not inventing arbitrary mechanisms — he's **reverse-engineering and systematizing** a known working clock.

### **DISCOVERY 3: FLINTLOCK MECHANISM DATING**

**Location:** f.18v-C

**German Commentary:**
> "Leonardos Verbesserungen des Luntenschlosses sind der Schnapp-Mechanismus und der automatisch öffnende Deckel der Pulverpfanne. Das Radschloss ersetzte die Lunte durch das Rad, das sich beim Auslösen drehte und dabei mit seiner Kante in der Pulverpfanne Funken erzeugte."

**English:**
> "Leonardo's improvements to the matchlock are the snap mechanism and the automatically opening cover of the powder pan. The wheel lock replaced the match with the wheel, which rotated when released and thereby produced sparks with its edge in the powder pan."

**CRITICAL CROSS-REFERENCE:**
> "Die 1974er Übersetzung für sscoppietto in der Überschrift gibt Büchse. Hier handelt es sich aber offensichtlich nicht um eine archaische Handkanone, sondern um eine Konstruktion mit Schloss, ähnlich der heute Arkebuse genannten Waffe."

**English:**
> "The 1974 translation for 'sscoppietto' in the heading gives 'musket'. However, this is obviously not an archaic hand cannon, but a construction with a lock, similar to the weapon now called an arquebus."

**DATING SIGNIFICANCE:**
The commentary notes that matchlock technology was "still a novelty in 1495" and that Leonardo's **automatic powder pan cover** and **snap-release trigger** represent significant advances. The wheel-lock (Radschloss) reference in Codex Atlanticus fol. 158r (56v-b) is dated to **~1500**, making Madrid I's matchlock mechanism **earlier**.

**CORRELATION WITH WHITE PAPER PRIORITY CLAIM:**
The white paper claims "complete flintlock prototype (c.1493-97, 115 years before le Bourgeoys)". The German commentary confirms the **automatic pan-cover mechanism** as Leonardo's innovation, though it describes it as a matchlock, not a flintlock.

**DISCREPANCY TO INVESTIGATE:**
- White paper: "flintlock"
- German commentary: "Luntenschloss" (matchlock) with automatic pan cover
- Is the white paper's "flintlock" claim accurate, or should it be "advanced matchlock"?

The **functional principle** (snap-trigger + automatic cover) is confirmed, but the **ignition method** (match vs. flint) needs clarification.
