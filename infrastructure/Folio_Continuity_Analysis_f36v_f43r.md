### **f36v Final Content**

**From Master Reconstruction Dossier:**

**f36v — Multiple transmissions with gears and tackles**

Leonardo's text:
```
"This method is similar to that of the levers shown opposite in equal number of bars. The only difference is this: being made with toothed wheels and their pinions, it has continuity in its motion, of which the simple lever instrument is deprived."

"If you multiply here the number of the little wheels or pulleys by the number of pounds of the counterweight f, you will find the weight m-n that stands in equilibrium with f, and it will be 33 pounds. And among compounded pulls this is the weakest and of least virtue, and is praised only because it is very safe, since it is not subject to wheel teeth like the others, although the instrument above could also be made with cords and then it too would be safe."
```

**Key Themes on f36v:**
- Gear trains versus tackles
- **Continuity of motion** (gears)  
- **Safety** (tackles/cords)
- Trade-off: continuous but tooth-dependent vs interrupted but safe
